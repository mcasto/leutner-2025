Carol Leutner’s memoir transcends the limitations of the genre. Her decades of commitment to racial and economic justice, personal battles on behalf of her family, and professional experience as a woman in often hostile environments span the globe. From rural outposts to national and international centers of power, she persevered and maintained a belief in the higher good. She has touched the lives of so many that this is more than an inspiring individual story. If it weren’t a true-life adventure, it would read like a thrilling soap opera.
— James P. Ito-Adler, Ph.D. Anthropologist, former professor at Florida International University, currently President, Cambridge Institute for Brazilian Studies
