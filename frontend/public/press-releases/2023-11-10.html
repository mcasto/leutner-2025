New Political Memoir sheds light on issues of race and gender prejudice and adversity facing modern America.

MegaShift Publishing, a boutique publisher based in the Washington D.C. area, is pleased to announce the release of Race Consciousness, A Personal and Political Journey.

In this political memoir, author Carol E. Leutner presents a narrative of discovery about racism and power from the 60s to the present that reflects many of the issues facing the United States today.

Leutner says, “Much of what people know about the “other,” persons different from ourselves, comes from third parties. This is particularly true regarding race, ethnicity and religion. We learned it from our parents, we heard it from someone, we read it somewhere, we viewed it on social media. Third-party information is trusted more than our own instincts.”

Carol Leutner’s story shows how one-on-one conversation, personal experience, and first-hand knowledge can help bridge our differences.

The author recognized how institutional structures based on race influenced economic development, when she worked with Indigenous and Hispanic communities in the American Southwest. A politically-active marriage to an African American revealed how entrenched race-and gender-based power structures truly are. Her book reveals the process by which she became aware of the conditions impacting her life.

This memoir also provides context by framing events that Leutner either witnessed or inspired from an historical perspective. It is difficult to explain the social, political and cultural difficulties that Americans now face without knowledge of past struggles, indeed many struggles.

Race Consciousness covers decades of personal and political struggles. It provides analysis and insight as to why both public and private decisions were made--context for how events unfolded as they did.

The book is also about raising one’s consciousness, opening the mind to greater awareness and understanding, and being respectful of new information and the interconnectedness of all things. Letting go of old boundaries and assumptions means changing the conversation. Humanity is at a juncture where new ways of talking and relating are critical.

Carol’s story can help reimagine a national dialogue where shared survival trumps differences---a necessary step to address the existential threats of artificial intelligence, pandemics, atomic warfare, and the climate emergency.
